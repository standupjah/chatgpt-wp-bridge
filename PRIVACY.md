## Privacy Policy — chatgpt-wp-bridge

This GPT is designed to publish content to a WordPress site using a self-hosted API bridge.

### What Data Is Sent

When you use this GPT to publish content, the following information may be sent to the configured bridge service:

- Post title
- HTML content or transcript content
- Optional metadata (status, content type, etc.)

This data is sent only when you explicitly request publishing.

---

### How Data Is Used

The data sent is used solely to:

- Create or update content on your WordPress site

No additional processing, profiling, or analysis is performed by the bridge.

---

### Data Storage

- The bridge itself does not store submitted content beyond what is required to process the request.
- Published content is stored on your WordPress site.
- Logs may be generated by the server (e.g., request logs), depending on your server configuration.

---

### Authentication & Security

- Requests are authenticated using a shared secret (`X-Bridge-Secret`)
- You are responsible for keeping this secret secure
- It is recommended to run the bridge behind HTTPS and a reverse proxy

---

### Third Parties

This GPT does not send data to third-party services beyond:

- Your own WordPress installation
- Your own hosted bridge server

OpenAI may process prompts as part of normal ChatGPT operation according to their policies.

---

### User Responsibility

You are responsible for:

- The content you choose to publish
- Ensuring no sensitive or private data is unintentionally sent
- Securing your WordPress credentials and bridge endpoint

---

### Changes

This privacy policy may be updated as the project evolves.
